**CALORIE TARGET:** **2,800 kcal** — Modified training day (~75 min, RPE 6.5 cap), ~10,700 steps already done. This is a moderate-intake recovery-leaning day. Sleep debt elevates cortisol and impairs glucose handling, so we're not under-eating (which worsens recovery), but we're not surplus-loading either since training load is capped. If your weight comes back >80 kg, scale to 3,000–3,100.

**Why not higher?** Chronic sleep <6h reduces insulin sensitivity ~30% and shifts appetite toward hyperphagia of refined carbs. Your body will *want* more calories than it can productively use today. We feed the work, not the cravings.

---

**MACROS:**

- **Protein: 150 g (2.0 g/kg)** — Elevated end of range because sleep deprivation increases muscle protein breakdown ~18%. Higher protein partially offsets this catabolic state.
  - Sources: 2 whole eggs + 3 whites breakfast, 150g chicken breast lunch, 170g salmon dinner, Greek yogurt snack, whey shake post-training.

- **Carbohydrates: 340 g (4.5 g/kg)** — Moderate. Enough to top off glycogen for the 75-min session and refill after, but not overshooting given capped intensity.
  - Timing: ~60g in pre-training meal (2h before), ~80g in post-training window, rest distributed across breakfast and dinner. Front-load carbs earlier in the day — sleep-restricted athletes handle glucose better in the AM than PM.

- **Fats: 85 g** — Moderate. Skew toward omega-3 and monounsaturated; sleep deprivation is pro-inflammatory and we counter that with food.
  - Sources: olive oil, avocado, salmon, walnuts, whole eggs.
